# The Greater Deliverance

**Date:** March 25, 2026

Throughout Scripture, the theme of deliverance runs like a scarlet thread — from the exodus out of Egypt to the rescue of Daniel from the lion's den. Each act of salvation pointed forward, foreshadowing something far greater yet to come.

The deliverance of Israel from Pharaoh's grip was mighty. The parting of the Red Sea was awe-inspiring. The return from Babylonian exile was cause for celebration. Yet all of these, as extraordinary as they were, served as shadows of a deeper, more permanent rescue.

## Accomplished Through His Own Blood

The greater deliverance was not accomplished through the strength of armies, the wisdom of kings, or the courage of prophets. It was accomplished through His own blood — a sacrifice willingly offered, once and for all.

Where the blood of lambs covered sin temporarily, His blood removed it eternally. Where the first Passover spared a nation from physical death, this greater Passover delivers all who believe from the power of sin and the sting of death itself.

This is the deliverance that every prior act of rescue was pointing to — not merely freedom from an earthly oppressor, but freedom from the deepest bondage of the human condition. It is a deliverance that no enemy can reverse, no time can erode, and no power can overcome.

## A Deliverance That Endures

What makes this deliverance greater is not only its cost but its permanence. It is finished, complete, and sufficient. Nothing needs to be added. Nothing can be taken away.

To the greater deliverance accomplished through His own blood — all of history bows.
